Example Client Usage Scenario
Use Case: Moderate Investor During Market Volatility

?? Client Profile
Name: Sarah
Age: 32
Goal: Long-term wealth growth
Capital: 20,000
Assets available: Stocks (NYSE/NASDAQ), ETFs, Crypto (via Binance)

STEP 1 - Client Onboarding
What the Client Sees
Sarah signs up and logs into the platform.
She answers basic questions:
* Investment horizon?
* Monthly income?
* Liquidity needs?
* Previous investing experience?

What Happens in the Backend
1. User Service
o Creates user record in PostgreSQL.
o Initializes empty portfolio.
2. Behavioral Profiling Agent
o Converts answers into structured metrics:
* Risk tolerance = 62/100
* Investment horizon = Long-term
* Liquidity need = Low
* Emotional volatility score = Moderate
3. Profile stored in database.

STEP 2 - Market Update Trigger
External Reality
The market is unstable:
* NASDAQ drops 3% in one week.
* Volatility increases 28%.
* Crypto correlation with tech stocks rises.

What Happens in the Backend
1. Market Data Layer
o Pulls new data from:
* NASDAQ
* New York Stock Exchange
* Binance
2. Feature Engineering Engine
o Computes:
* Rolling volatility
* Correlation matrix
* Momentum indicators
* Drawdown metrics
3. Regime Detection Model
o Classifies market as:
? "High Volatility / Correction Regime"
4. Signals stored in Feature Store.

STEP 3 - Dynamic Adaptive Questioning (DAQ Agent)
What the Client Sees
Instead of static questions, Sarah receives:
"Tech markets have experienced increased volatility this week.
If your portfolio temporarily drops 15%, what would you most likely do?"
A) Sell immediately
B) Hold
C) Buy more
Then:
"Your profile suggests long-term growth.
Would you consider increasing exposure during market dips?"

What Happens in the Backend
1. DAQ Agent receives:
o Market regime = High volatility
o User risk score = 62
o Asset exposure potential = Tech-heavy risk
2. It detects:
o Risk score is moderate
o Market stress elevated
o Clarification needed
3. It generates context-aware behavioral stress questions.
4. User answers:
o "Hold"
o "Maybe increase slightly"
5. Behavioral Profiling Agent updates:
o Loss aversion coefficient ?
o Confidence level ?
o Risk tolerance adjusted to 68/100
This is adaptive behavioral modeling.

STEP 4 - Quantitative Portfolio Computation
What Happens in the Backend
1. Quant Engine receives:
o Forecasted volatility
o Correlation matrix
o User risk score (68)
o Investment horizon (long-term)
2. Portfolio Optimization Engine runs:
o Mean-Variance Optimization
o Risk constraint: max 18% volatility
o Monte Carlo stress test
3. Proposed allocation:
* 45% US Equities (diversified)
* 20% Global ETF
* 15% Crypto
* 15% Bonds ETF
* 5% Cash

STEP 5 - Risk Guardian Validation
Before showing results:
1. Risk Agent calculates:
o Value at Risk (95%)
o Expected shortfall
o Maximum drawdown projection
2. Finds:
o Crypto exposure slightly increases downside risk in stress scenario.
3. Adjusts allocation:
Crypto reduced from 15% ? 12%
Bonds increased from 15% ? 18%
This ensures constraint compliance.

STEP 6 - Explainability Generation
What the Client Sees
Sarah receives a report:
"Given increased market volatility and your improved tolerance for short-term fluctuations, the system recommends a diversified allocation emphasizing US equities and global ETFs.
Crypto exposure is limited to 12% due to correlation risk with technology stocks.
The expected annualized volatility is 14%, within your risk tolerance range."
She also sees:
* Risk heatmap
* Monte Carlo scenario graph
* Stress test outcome

What Happens in the Backend
1. Explainability Agent:
o Converts numerical output into human narrative.
o Adds risk disclosure.
o Generates summary PDF.
2. Report stored in reporting-service.

STEP 7 - Optional Execution
If Sarah connected her brokerage:
* The system prepares order list.
* Sends via secure API.
* Funds remain external.
If not:
* It simply provides allocation guidance.
